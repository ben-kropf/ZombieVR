About ZombieVR

ZombieVR is a virtual reality horror game where players must explore a procedurally generated environment in order to escape, all while avoiding flesh eating zombies. Inspired by other popular horror style games such as Amnesia and Slender, players are given a flashlight and must find weapons to defend themselves. Virtual reality brings an additional level of immersion that is sure to be an unforgettable experience.

ZombieVR was created using the Unity game engine and was desgined to be used with the Oculus Rift. Below are some screenshots of the game in action.

Development

As part of the process, we documented two checkpoints we crossed on our way to developing the finished product. Those checkpoints, as well as some details about each build, are described below.

Checkpoint 1

Andrew and Vincent developed the algorithm that would procedurally generate the maze. The maze will be different each time, making each playthrough a new experiance for the player. This will be the core foundation of our game. Ben worked to find some models to be used for zombies and other game components. He also worked on developing a toggleable flashlight that the player will carry as their main source of light.

Austin developed the scripts for the player movements. They provide a first person controller that can walk, run, and jump with natural head bob, and audio clips for walking and jumping. The first person camera is currently controlled using a mouse, but will later be attached to a VR camera for the Rift. The player can also shoot projectiles with an adjustable speed/bullet type and aim with the GUI crosshairs.

Checkpoint 2

Ben worked on the script to open and close doors. This will allow the player to explore doors and enter some of the rooms that were procedurally generated. Vincent worked on spawning zombies at random locations around the procedurally generated level. These zombies are only spawned away from the player so the player has a chance to orient themselves before being attacked. Andrew developed a script that moved the zombies towards the player once they are within a certain range. This allows the zombies to chase after the player. Austin worked on player zombie interaction, mainly the zombie taking damage and being destroyed by shooting projectiles at it.
